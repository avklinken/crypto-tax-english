## How Cryptocurrency is Generally Taxed

In most jurisdictions, cryptocurrencies are considered a form of property for tax purposes. This means that typical tax rules applicable to property transactions also apply to crypto transactions. Here's a quick breakdown:

- **Buying Cryptocurrency**: Purchasing cryptocurrency with fiat currency is generally not a taxable event.
- **Selling Cryptocurrency**: Selling your crypto for fiat currency or exchanging it for another cryptocurrency is a taxable event.
- **Mining**: Cryptocurrencies obtained through mining are taxable as income when you receive them.

However, what happens when you simply hold onto your cryptocurrency without selling it?

## Tax Implications of Holding Cryptocurrency

If you merely hold onto your cryptocurrency and do not sell it, you typically do not incur a taxable event. This means you do not have to pay taxes on the cryptocurrency simply for holding it. However, it is important to keep detailed records of your transactions.
