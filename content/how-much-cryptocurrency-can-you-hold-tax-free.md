<h2>Understanding Tax-Free Crypto Holdings</h2>
<p>In most jurisdictions, cryptocurrencies are treated as property for tax purposes, which means they are subject to capital gains taxes. However, the amount of cryptocurrency you can hold without incurring taxes largely depends on the specific laws of your country and your personal financial situation.</p>
<h3>Personal Allowances and Thresholds</h3>
<p>Many countries offer a personal allowance or threshold for capital gains, which allows individuals to earn a certain amount before they have to pay taxes. For example, in the United States, the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) offers a capital gains tax exemption for gains up to a specific limit. This means that if your crypto gains are below this threshold, you might not owe any taxes.</p>
